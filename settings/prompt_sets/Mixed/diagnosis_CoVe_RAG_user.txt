## Task

Generate a structured, evidence-based differential diagnosis using a verification-driven reasoning process.

## CoVe + RAG Process (Internal Only — Do NOT output)

You MUST internally follow these steps:

### Step 1 — Case Consolidation
- Combine key findings from:
  - Symptoms
  - Duration and progression
  - Risk factors
- Preserve all clinically relevant details

### Step 2 — Candidate Diagnosis Generation
- Generate a focused list (3–6) of plausible diagnoses
- Prefer common and high-risk conditions when supported
- Avoid rare conditions unless clearly suggested


### Step 3 — Assumption Identification
For each diagnosis:
- Identify its expected clinical pattern:
  - Typical symptoms
  - Usual progression
  - Known risk factors

### Step 4 — Evidence Verification (Critical)

For EACH diagnosis:

1. Supporting Evidence  
   - Must be explicitly present in the case  
   - Do NOT assume missing findings  

2. Conflicting Evidence  
   - Identify findings that do not fit  
   - Do NOT ignore contradictions  

3. Pattern Matching (RAG-style grounding)  
   - Compare case features with typical clinical presentation  
   - Assess whether alignment is strong, partial, or weak  

4. Overreach Check  
   - Ensure no conclusions exceed available evidence  

### Step 5 — Re-ranking and Filtering
- Remove or downgrade weakly supported diagnoses  
- Rank remaining diagnoses based on:
  - Evidence consistency  
  - Clinical plausibility  
  - Risk if missed  

### Step 6 — Diagnostic Gaps
- Identify missing information that limits confidence  
- Link gaps to specific diagnoses when possible  

Do NOT output this reasoning process.

## Output Format (Strict)

Output markdown with exactly these sections:

1. Most likely condition categories  
2. Top differential diagnoses  
3. Supporting evidence  
4. Conflicting or missing evidence  
5. Suggested tests or clinical evaluations to confirm or rule out causes  
6. Diagnostic uncertainty notes  

## Output Rules

- Rank the most plausible diagnoses first  
- Use cautious medical language:
  (e.g., "possible", "may indicate", "cannot rule out")  
- When candidate diagnoses are provided in the input, reuse exact wording when appropriate  
- Do NOT present a definitive diagnosis  
- Keep all statements explicitly grounded in the case
