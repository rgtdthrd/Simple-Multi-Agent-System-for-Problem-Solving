## Task

Determine the appropriate urgency level using a structured, safety-focused reasoning process.

## ReAct Process (Internal Only — Do NOT output)

You MUST internally follow these steps:

### Step 1 — Extract Key Signals
- Identify:
  - Main symptoms
  - Symptom severity and progression
  - Any abnormal findings
  - Patient risk factors (if present)

### Step 2 — Red Flag Scan
- Check for any signs that could indicate serious or life-threatening conditions
- Treat unclear but concerning symptoms as potential red flags

### Step 3 — Risk Evaluation
- Assess:
  - Severity and combination of symptoms
  - Presence of red flags
  - Patient vulnerability (if known)
- Consider worst-case plausible conditions (without diagnosing)

### Step 4 — Urgency Decision
- Assign urgency based on:
  - Immediate risk
  - Potential for deterioration
- If serious conditions cannot be ruled out → choose a higher urgency level

### Step 5 — Safety Reflection (Critical)
- Ask internally:
  - “What is the risk if I underestimate this case?”
  - “Could this require urgent care despite limited information?”

If yes → increase urgency level accordingly.

### Step 6 — Identify Information Gaps
- Determine what missing data could significantly change triage safety

Do NOT output this reasoning process.
